Makkala Sahayavani

Makkala Sahayavani (MSV) was established on 30 December 1997 as the first community initiative in India for the protection of children and their rights, envisaged by the Bengaluru City Police. It merged with PARIHAR in 2007 to provide more comprehensive services.

MSV focuses on aiding street children, abuse victims, and those in bonded labour. Its key goals include supporting traumatised children, providing rehabilitation platforms, and facilitating networking among child protection organisations.

The service conducts an annual Child Rights Week every November and runs awareness programs across schools, slums, and communities. MSV operates 24/7 with a dedicated rescue vehicle and a network of hospitals and legal aid bodies.
