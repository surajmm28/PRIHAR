Family Counselling Centre

The Family Counselling Centre (FCC) was the first government-established family counselling centre in Karnataka, set up in 1993 to prevent family-based violence and address the root causes of domestic disputes. It operates under PARIHAR and is supported by the Central Social Welfare Board, New Delhi.

The FCC recognises that "most of the atrocities on women originate from dysfunctional families" due to globalisation, consumerism, and socio-economic pressures. It aims to address family conflicts through intensive counselling — strengthening family bonds and resolving disputes amicably.

The centre is overseen by the Karnataka State Social Welfare Board and supports entire families — not only women — in a professional, confidential, and non-judgmental environment.
