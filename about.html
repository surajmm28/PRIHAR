About Us

Overview

PARIHAR, an initiative by the Bengaluru City Police, is a charitable institution registered under the Karnataka Societies Registration Act of 1960. It is a non-profit organization established in 1998, steered by a dedicated team of professional counsellors and police personnel.

PARIHAR is committed to the welfare of women, children, and families through counselling and a range of charitable, socio-economic programs. The organization provides multidisciplinary support — counselling, police support, shelter, medical care, psychological counselling, legal assistance, and skill development — all free of cost.

Over the past 30 years, PARIHAR has worked relentlessly to reinforce family values and resolve disputes amicably. Its Head Office is at the Command Center, Office of the Commissioner of Police, Ali Asker Road, Bengaluru. It operates eight counselling centres across Women Police Stations in every zone of the city.

What We Do

Our Services

Eight comprehensive, free-of-charge services for women, children, and families in distress.

Counselling

Qualified professional counsellors help beneficiaries to solve family issues through proper guidance and counselling sessions.

Police Support

Assistance for women and children in accessing police support, intervention, and protection when needed urgently.

Medical & Psychological Assistance

PARIHAR provides necessary medical and psychiatric assistance to women, children, and families in distress.

Short Stay Home (Shelter)

Partnership arrangements with government bodies and NGOs to provide emergency housing and short-stay facilities.

Child Welfare Committee

Rescued children are referred to government-run children's homes managed by the Child Welfare Committee for care and rehabilitation.

Legal Aid Assistance

Free legal support through Karnataka State Legal Services Authority, including weekly legal consultations every Wednesday.

Awareness Programs & Workshops

Educational workshops for schools, colleges, workplaces, and communities on women's rights, child safety, and family welfare.

Skill Development Training

Three training centres in partnership with Bengaluru City Police offering tailoring, E-auto driving, and vocational skills for women.
